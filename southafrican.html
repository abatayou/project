Short description

The South African accent is not a single standard, but a variety of variations that reflect the country's cultural and linguistic heritage. Unlike the American "General American" accent, which is considered a neutral standard, there is no single "neutral" accent in South Africa. Instead, each accent is associated with the history and community from which it originates.
